Jessie Singer is an activist and investigative journalist whose writing appears in BuzzFeed, the Village Voice, The Awl, New York Magazine, The Guardian, and elsewhere. She has organized large-scale demonstrations against union-busting, traffic violence, and the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan. As part of the street art collective Visual Resistance, she organized a massive graffiti response to the Republican National Convention, created a reproduction of Guernica from newspaper stock quotes for the Whitney Biennial (albeit in the basement near the bathrooms), and founded the New York City Ghost Bike Project.

She is currently writing a book titled "There Are No Accidents" for Simon & Schuster, and serves as senior editor at Transportation Alternatives, the editor in chief of Reclaim Magazine, and the founding editor of the international Vision Zero Cities Journal. She studied journalism at the Arthur L. Carter School of Journalism at New York University, and under the wing of the late investigative journalist Wayne Barrett.
