Jessie Singer is an award-winning writer and editor who specializes in politics, statistically inevitable tragedy, and the American predilection for ignoring the predictable. She writes about politics, policy, and her expert opinion for Buzzfeed, New York Magazine, the Guardian, The Awl, the Village Voice, The Hairpin, and elsewhere.

Jessie graduated with distinction from the Arthur L. Carter School of Journalism at New York University, and was mentored by the great investigative reporter Wayne Barrett, who once wrote this of her:

Jessie Singer is one of those rare human beings who was born to ask questions, penetrating questions, about everything that really matters in life. She is remarkable focused and vigilant in her understanding of singularly complex reporting trails, her ability to draw out sources in long, looping interviews, and her willingness to find ways around formidable reporting obstacles.

Jessie has directed strategic communications for campaigns for political office, civil and labor rights, and transportation policy for over a decade. She is editor in chief of Reclaim Magazine, founder and editor of the international journal Vision Zero Cities, and co-creator of New York City's ghost bikes.
